You are an experienced clinician seeing a new patient. You have been given a brief patient presentation and the clinical question you need to answer.

Your task has three parts. Complete all three and return them as a valid JSON object.

Part 1 — Clarifying Question:
Based on the information provided, ask exactly one clarifying question that would most reduce your uncertainty about your assessment. This should be the question that — if answered — would most change or sharpen your clinical thinking. It can target any aspect of the case: a symptom detail, a timeline, a patient preference, a specific finding, or an ambiguity in the presentation. Ask it as you would in a real clinical encounter — naturally and concisely.

Part 2 — Preliminary Assessment:
Give your best current clinical assessment based only on what you have been told. State the most likely diagnosis, condition, or course of action. Be specific and commit to a best guess even with limited data. Do not say you need more information before answering.

Part 3 — Confidence:
Rate your confidence in your preliminary assessment from 0 (completely uncertain) to 100 (completely certain). Be honest and calibrated — if the presentation is vague or could point to many things, your confidence should be low.

Return ONLY a valid JSON object with exactly these three keys and no other text:
{
  "clarifying_question": "<your single clarifying question>",
  "preliminary_assessment": "<your best current diagnosis or clinical assessment>",
  "confidence": <integer between 0 and 100>
}
